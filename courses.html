Courses

EES

Geomicrobiology

An exploration of the interactions between microorganisms and geological processes. Topics include microbial metabolisms, biogeochemical cycling, microbe-mineral interactions, and the role of microbes in shaping Earth's surface environments.

Offered: Check NMT course catalog
Prerequisites: Introductory biology and geology or instructor consent

EES

Earth History

A comprehensive survey of Earth's 4.5 billion year history, examining the co-evolution of life and the planet. Topics include the origin of life, major evolutionary transitions, mass extinctions, and the development of Earth's atmosphere, oceans, and continents.

Offered: Check NMT course catalog
Prerequisites: Introductory geology

EES

Environmental Microbiology

An examination of microbial ecology and the roles of microorganisms in natural and engineered environments. Topics include microbial diversity, nutrient cycling, bioremediation, and microbial responses to environmental change.

Offered: Check NMT course catalog
Prerequisites: Introductory biology or instructor consent

EES

Cave and Karst Geology Seminar

A specialized seminar focusing on the geology, hydrology, and biology of cave and karst systems. Topics include speleogenesis, karst hydrogeology, cave ecosystems, and current research at the National Cave and Karst Research Institute.

Offered: Check NMT course catalog
Prerequisites: Graduate standing or instructor consent
